**Appendix — Backend Rules, Triggers & Scoring**

This section outlines the backend rules, triggers, and scoring mechanisms governing Banking Intelligence initiatives, focusing on feature definitions, actionable rules, propensity scoring, and the next-best-action priority stack. The aim is to provide a technical blueprint for system implementation and strategic decision-making within the banking platform.

### Feature Definitions
Core features are derived from transactional data and customer behavior, serving as foundational inputs for rule evaluation and scoring models.

* **avg_daily_spend**: Represents the average daily outflow, calculated as `total_outflows / 33` over a defined period (e.g., last 33 days).
* **liquidity_floor**: A dynamic threshold representing a recommended minimum balance, defined as `2 × avg_daily_spend` and calculated per cycle. This acts as a predictive low-balance indicator.
* **dk_ratio_other**: The ratio of debits originating from a specific category ("dk_debits") to other subtractions, calculated as `|dk_debits| / |other_subtractions|`.
* **dk_ratio_total**: The ratio of debits from a specific category ("dk_debits") to total outflows, calculated as `|dk_debits| / total_outflows`.
* **coffee_freq, transit_freq, rideshare_freq**: Keyword counts per cycle, indicating the frequency of transactions related to specific categories (e.g., coffee shops, public transport, ride-sharing services).

### Example Rules (JSON-like)
Rules are defined with clear `if-then` conditions, triggering specific actions upon fulfillment. These rules are designed for automated execution within the banking system.

```json
{
"liquidity_guardrail": {
"if": "end_balance < 1.5 * avg_daily_spend",
"then": ["enable_overdraft_grace", "offer_loc_1500"]
}
}
```

* **liquidity_guardrail**: This rule triggers when the customer's `end_balance` falls below 1.5 times their `avg_daily_spend`. Upon activation, it initiates two actions: `enable_overdraft_grace` (providing a short-term buffer against overdraft fees) and `offer_loc_1500` (presenting a pre-approved Line of Credit up to $1500).

### Scoring Sketch
Scoring models utilize defined features to calculate propensity scores, indicating the likelihood of a customer engaging with specific products or services. These scores drive personalized offers and intelligent automation.

* **propensity_auto_loan**: Calculated as `sigmoid(0.8 * transit_freq + 0.5 * rideshare_freq - 1.2 * has_auto_loan)`. This model evaluates the likelihood of a customer needing an auto loan based on their transit and rideshare frequencies, negatively weighted by whether they already possess an auto loan (`has_auto_loan`).
* **propensity_coffee_rewards**: Calculated as `sigmoid(0.7 * coffee_freq + 0.3 * restaurants_freq)`. This model assesses the likelihood of a customer engaging with a coffee rewards program, based on their frequency of coffee-related and general restaurant transactions.

### Next-Best-Action (Priority Stack)
The next-best-action priority stack dictates the sequence of interventions or offers presented to a customer, ordered by strategic importance and potential impact.

1. **Liquidity guardrail (overdraft grace + LOC)**: This action is prioritized to proactively address potential liquidity issues, prevent account attrition due to insufficient funds, and offer immediate financial solutions.
2. **Coffee rewards activation**: Focused on driving daily engagement and encouraging frequent use of banking services tied to lifestyle spending.
3. **Default action — placeholder**: A generic placeholder for a fallback or general engagement action when higher-priority triggers are not met or specific conditions are not applicable.
